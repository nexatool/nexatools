Tips for Getting the Best Results

The right settings depend on your image type and what you'll use it for. Here's how to get the smallest file without losing noticeable quality:

1. Use JPG for Photos

Photographs with lots of color detail compress best as JPG. A quality setting around 70 to 80 usually looks nearly identical to the original at a fraction of the size.

2. Use PNG for Graphics With Transparency

Logos, icons and screenshots with sharp edges or transparency look best as PNG. Since PNG is lossless, expect smaller size savings than JPG or WebP.

3. Try WebP for the Smallest File Size

WebP typically produces the smallest files at a given quality level and is supported by all modern browsers, making it a great choice for websites.

4. Compare Before You Download

Use the before-and-after preview and the percentage saved to confirm the result still looks good before you replace your original file.
