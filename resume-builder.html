Resume Writing Tips for 2026

A great resume builder is only as good as the content you put in it. Here are the most important tips to make your resume stand out:

1. Write a Strong Summary First

Your summary or objective statement is the first thing recruiters read. Be specific about your skills and what you offer. Use our AI Summary Writer to generate a professional starting point, then customize it with your unique experience.

2. Use Action Verbs

Every bullet point should start with a strong action verb: Built, Designed, Led, Increased, Reduced, Launched, Managed. Avoid passive language like "was responsible for."

3. Quantify Your Achievements

Numbers make your experience real. "Increased sales by 30%" is far more powerful than "helped improve sales." Add percentages, dollar amounts, or time saved wherever possible.

4. Choose the Right Template for Your Industry

For corporate and traditional roles, use Classic or Executive. For tech and developer roles, Modern or Neon Dark work best. For creative roles, Creative or Bold templates make a strong impression. For ATS systems, stick to Minimal or Classic.

5. Keep It to One Page

Fresh graduates and professionals with under 7 years of experience should keep their resume to one page. Be concise — every line should earn its place.

6. Add LinkedIn and GitHub Links

Use the LinkedIn and GitHub fields to add your profile URLs. Recruiters will visit these links to learn more about you. Make sure your profiles are up to date before applying.
