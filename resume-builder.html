Resume Writing Tips for Pakistani Job Market 2026

A great resume builder is only as good as the content you put in it. Here are tips specific to Pakistani recruiters and job portals:

1. Use Formal Mode for Govt and Banking Jobs

For PPSC, FPSC, NTS, and banking sector applications, enable Formal Mode. These employers expect Father's Name, CNIC, Domicile, and Marital Status on your CV. For tech startups and multinationals, keep it off.

2. Fresh Grads: Education Comes First

In Pakistan, fresh graduates and internship applicants should list education before work experience. Use Internship Mode which automatically places education at the top of your CV.

3. Include Certifications Prominently

Pakistani employers value certifications highly. Include ACCA, CFA, PMP, CCNA, Oracle, or any Coursera/Google certificates. List them in the Certifications section with the year.

4. CGPA Matters — Include It

Most Pakistani employers ask for CGPA for fresh graduates. Include your CGPA alongside your degree. If it is below 3.0, you may choose to include only your percentage instead.

5. References Are Expected

Unlike Western CVs, Pakistani employers expect references. Include at least one professional and one academic reference, or write "Available on request" if you prefer.

6. Optimize for Rozee.pk and LinkedIn

Classic and Minimal templates work best when uploaded to Rozee.pk or LinkedIn. They are clean, ATS-friendly, and readable in both digital and printed formats.
