How to Convert PNG to JPG and JPG to PNG Online

📅 June 23, 2026 ⏱ 6 min read ✍️ NexaTools Team

You have a PNG screenshot that is too heavy for an email, or a JPG photo that needs a transparent background for a design project. Converting between PNG and JPG solves both problems, and it takes seconds once you know what to expect from each direction.

Converting PNG to JPG

This is usually done to reduce file size. PNGs preserve every pixel exactly, which makes them large especially for photographs. Converting to JPG applies lossy compression, shrinking the file significantly. The one thing to know: JPG does not support transparency, so any transparent areas in your PNG will be filled with a solid background color usually white during conversion.

Converting JPG to PNG

This is usually done when you need transparency, or when you are preparing an image for further editing where lossless quality matters for example, adding a logo on top of a background in a design tool. Converting JPG to PNG does not restore any detail lost during the original JPG compression, but it does prevent further quality loss during additional edits and re-saves.

When to Convert PNG → JPG
Reducing email or website file size, sharing photos where transparency is not needed, printing standard photographs.
When to Convert JPG → PNG
Adding transparency for design work, preparing an image for further lossless editing, creating icons or overlays.

✦ Watch for Transparency Loss
Before converting a PNG with a transparent logo to JPG, double check where it will be used. If it needs to sit over different colored backgrounds later, keep it as PNG or convert to WebP instead, since WebP supports transparency while still compressing smaller than PNG.

Batch Converting Multiple Images

If you have a folder of mixed PNG and JPG files that all need to be one consistent format for example, preparing a product catalog convert them in a batch rather than one at a time. Keep the same target format and quality setting across the batch so your final set of images looks consistent.

After Converting: Resize and Compress
