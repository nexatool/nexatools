How to Build an ATS-Friendly Resume

Many employers use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to scan resumes before a human ever sees them. Keeping your resume simple, well-structured and keyword-relevant helps it pass those automated filters and reach a hiring manager. A few practical habits make a real difference:

  • Keep your resume to one or two pages.
  • Tailor it for each job application.
  • Use clear section headings.
  • Highlight measurable achievements.
  • Save as PDF before submitting.
