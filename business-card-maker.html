Business Card Design Tips for 2026

A great business card does more than share your contact info it makes people remember you. Here's how to design one that works:

Keep It Simple and Readable

Avoid putting too much information on your card. Include only what matters most: your name, title, company, phone, and email. Less is more — a clean, readable card is always more professional than a cluttered one.

Use High Contrast Colors

Ensure your text is always clearly readable by maintaining high contrast between your background and text colors. Dark backgrounds with light text, or white backgrounds with dark text, are the safest choices for readability both on screen and in print.

Choose the Right Font

Use clean, professional fonts like sans-serif styles (General Sans, Arial) for modern looks, or serif fonts (Georgia) for classic, elegant styles. Avoid decorative or script fonts that may be hard to read at small print sizes.

Include Your Social or Website

In 2026, a website URL or LinkedIn profile is as important as a phone number. Include one key online presence so people can find your portfolio, services, or professional profile after the meeting.

Standard Business Card Dimensions

The global standard business card size is 3.5 × 2 inches (88.9 × 50.8 mm), or 85 × 55 mm in most of Asia and Europe. Our tool exports at a resolution suitable for professional printing at any standard print shop or online printer.

Digital vs Printed Cards

Download your card as a PNG to share digitally via WhatsApp, email, LinkedIn, or as a phone wallpaper. For physical cards, take your PNG to any print shop — Vistaprint, Canva Print, or local print shops all accept PNG files for business card printing.
