Best Image Resizer for Social Media (Instagram, Facebook & LinkedIn)

📅 June 21, 2026 ⏱ 7 min read ✍️ NexaTools Team

Ever posted a photo that looked perfect on your phone, only to have it cropped awkwardly once it hit Instagram or LinkedIn? Every social platform expects images at specific dimensions, and uploading the wrong size means the platform crops or stretches your content automatically, often cutting off exactly the part you wanted visible.

Why Image Dimensions Matter on Social Media

Each platform's feed, story, and profile layout is built around fixed aspect ratios. When your uploaded image does not match, the platform's algorithm decides how to crop it and it does not always crop where you would choose. Resizing your image to the correct dimensions before uploading puts you back in control of how your content appears.

Instagram
Feed posts: 1080x1080 (square) or 1080x1350 (portrait). Stories and Reels: 1080x1920. Profile picture: 320x320.
Facebook
Shared image posts: 1200x630. Cover photo: 820x312. Profile picture: 170x170.
LinkedIn
Shared image posts: 1200x627. Company banner: 1128x191. Profile picture: 400x400.

✦ Keep the Subject Centered
When resizing a photo to a different aspect ratio, keep the main subject a face, product, or logo centered in the frame. This gives you more flexibility if the platform later crops the image differently for feed vs. thumbnail display.

Resizing Without Stretching or Distorting

The most common mistake is forcing an image into a size that does not match its original aspect ratio, which stretches faces and objects into odd proportions. Always resize by cropping to fit the new ratio rather than stretching the existing image. NexaTools handles this automatically by letting you adjust the crop area rather than blindly forcing new dimensions.

Platform-Specific Tips

  • Instagram Stories keep important text and faces within the middle 60% of the frame, since the top and bottom areas are often covered by UI elements
  • Facebook cover photos preview on both desktop and mobile before finalizing, since the crop differs slightly between the two
  • LinkedIn banners avoid placing text near the edges, since your profile photo overlaps part of the banner
  • Thumbnails and previews test how your resized image looks at a small size, not just full screen

Resize, Then Compress

Once your image is the correct size, run it through the Image Compressor tool before uploading. Platforms recompress images anyway resizing and compressing beforehand gives you control over quality rather than leaving it entirely to the platform's algorithm.
