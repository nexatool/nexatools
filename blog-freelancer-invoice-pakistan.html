How to Send a Professional Invoice as a Freelancer in Pakistan Complete Guide 2026

📅 June 14, 2026 ⏱ 8 min read ✍️ NexaTools Team
🧾

One of the biggest challenges for Pakistani freelancers especially those working on Upwork, Fiverr, or with direct clients is getting paid properly and on time. A professional invoice is not just a payment request. It is a legal document that protects you, builds trust with your client, and makes your freelance business look serious.

This guide covers everything you need to know about creating and sending invoices as a Pakistani freelancer whether your client is in Lahore, London, or Los Angeles.

Why Pakistani Freelancers Need Proper Invoices

Many freelancers in Pakistan simply send a WhatsApp message saying "please send payment" and then wonder why clients delay or dispute amounts. A proper invoice solves all of this:

  • Creates a written record of the work done and amount agreed
  • Gives the client clear payment instructions and deadline
  • Makes your business look professional and trustworthy
  • Helps you track income for tax purposes
  • Reduces payment disputes everything is in writing
  • Required by international clients for their accounting records

What Every Freelance Invoice Must Include

Invoice Number
Every invoice needs a unique number INV-001, INV-002, and so on. This helps both you and your client track payments. Never send two invoices with the same number.
Your Name and Contact Details
Your full name or business name, email address, phone number, and city. For international clients, include your country Pakistan. If you have a website or portfolio, add that too.
Client Name and Details
The full name or company name of the client, their email, and their country. This makes the invoice legally valid and shows professionalism.
Invoice Date and Due Date
Always include both. Invoice date is when you issued it. Due date is when payment is expected. Standard for Pakistani freelancers is Net 7 (7 days) or Net 15 (15 days). International clients often expect Net 30.
Services Description
List exactly what work you did. "Freelance work" is not enough. Write: "Logo design 3 concepts, 2 revision rounds" or "WordPress website development 5 pages with contact form." The more specific, the fewer disputes.
Amount and Currency
State the currency clearly PKR, USD, GBP, EUR. For international clients always invoice in USD or their local currency. Never assume confusion about currency leads to underpayment.
Payment Method
Tell the client exactly how to pay. Include your bank account details, Easypaisa/JazzCash number, PayPal email, Wise account, or Payoneer details whichever applies.
Total Amount Due
Make the total bold and clear. If there are multiple line items, show subtotal, any taxes or discounts, and the final total separately.

Payment Methods for Pakistani Freelancers

This is where Pakistani freelancers have unique challenges. Not all international payment methods are available in Pakistan. Here is what actually works:

✦ For International Clients (USD/EUR/GBP)
Payoneer most popular for Pakistani freelancers. Accepted by Upwork, Fiverr, Freelancer. Withdraw to your Pakistani bank account in PKR.

Wise (formerly TransferWise) excellent for direct clients. Fast transfers, low fees, real exchange rates.

PayPal limited in Pakistan. You can receive payments but withdrawal options are restricted. Use with caution.

Crypto (USDT) increasingly used by Pakistani freelancers for direct international payments. Fast and low fees.
✦ For Local Pakistani Clients (PKR)
Bank Transfer most professional. Share your account title, account number, and IBAN.

Easypaisa / JazzCash fast and convenient for smaller amounts.

Raast Pakistan's instant payment system. Share your Raast ID for instant transfers between banks.

How to Handle Taxes on Your Invoice

Pakistani freelancers earning from international clients are generally exempt from sales tax on exported services but you should still be aware of your obligations:

  • FBR registration if your annual income exceeds Rs. 600,000, you are required to file a tax return in Pakistan
  • Export of services income from foreign clients is considered export income and may have tax incentives under Pakistani law
  • Withholding tax some Pakistani clients may deduct withholding tax. Ask them to provide a tax deduction certificate
  • GST generally not applicable for exported services, but consult a local tax advisor for your specific situation

Common Invoicing Mistakes Pakistani Freelancers Make

  • No due date without a due date, clients feel no urgency to pay. Always include one.
  • Vague service description "Design work" is not enough. Be specific about exactly what was delivered.
  • Wrong currency always confirm currency with client before starting work. State it clearly on the invoice.
  • No invoice number makes tracking payments impossible. Always number your invoices.
  • Sending invoice as image always send as PDF. Images can be edited and are not professional.
  • No follow up if payment is not received by due date, send a polite reminder within 2-3 days.
  • No late fee clause consider adding "Late payments subject to 2% monthly fee" to encourage on-time payment.

How to Follow Up on Unpaid Invoices

Late payments are common in freelancing both locally and internationally. Here is how to handle them professionally:

✦ Follow Up Email Template
Subject: Payment Reminder Invoice #INV-001

Hi [Client Name],

I hope you are doing well. I wanted to follow up on Invoice #INV-001 for [service] dated [date], which was due on [due date]. The total amount outstanding is [amount].

Please let me know if you have any questions or if there is anything I can help clarify. I would appreciate payment at your earliest convenience.

Thank you,
[Your Name]

Send the first reminder 1-2 days after the due date. If no response after 7 days, send a second reminder with a firmer tone. After 14 days, escalate if needed especially for international clients where you may need to open a dispute on the platform.
