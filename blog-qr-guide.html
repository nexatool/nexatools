How to Create QR Codes for Your Business — Free in 2026

📅 May 2026 ⏱ 5 min read ✍️ NexaTools Team

QR codes went from being a niche technology to a daily necessity. Restaurants use them for menus, shops use them for payments, freelancers use them on business cards, and event organizers use them for check-ins. In 2026, if your business does not have a QR code, you are missing a simple and free opportunity to connect with people faster.

This guide explains what QR codes can do, the different types available, and how to create them for free in under a minute.

What Can a QR Code Link To?

A QR code is just a scannable container for any kind of digital information. When someone scans it with their phone camera, it takes them directly to whatever you put inside. Here are the most useful types for businesses and individuals:

🔗
Website / URL
Link directly to your website, portfolio, product page, or any online destination. The most common use case. Put this QR code on your business card, flyer, or storefront.
📶
WiFi Credentials
Let customers or guests connect to your WiFi by scanning a code — no need to read out a long password. Perfect for cafes, offices, waiting rooms, and hotels.
💳
UPI / Payment
Generate a QR code with your UPI ID so customers can pay you instantly by scanning. Widely used by small shops, street vendors, and service providers in Pakistan, India, and beyond.
📞
Phone Number
Scanning calls or saves your number automatically. Great for business cards and printed materials where you want people to reach you without typing a number.
📧
Email Address
Opens a pre-filled email to your address when scanned. Useful for contact pages, printed materials, and anywhere you want people to email you easily.
📝
Plain Text / Menu
Display any text when scanned — a restaurant menu, a product description, an address, or a short message. No internet connection required for the scanner.

Real Business Uses for QR Codes in Pakistan

  • Restaurants and dhabas: Print a menu QR code on a table card — saves printing costs and lets you update the menu anytime by changing the link
  • Freelancers: Add a QR code to your business card linking to your portfolio or LinkedIn profile
  • Shops and retail: Accept payments via QR code linked to your Easypaisa, JazzCash, or bank account
  • Teachers and tutors: Share course materials or contact details via QR code in your classroom
  • Event organizers: Registration forms, event pages, or schedule links via QR codes on banners

Do QR Codes Expire?

Static QR codes — which is what NexaTools generates — never expire. The information is encoded directly in the QR code image. As long as the destination (like a website URL) is still working, the QR code will work forever. There are no subscriptions or renewals required.

Can I Customize the QR Code Color?

Yes. NexaTools lets you choose from several colors for your QR code. The key rule is to keep strong contrast between the QR code color and the background. Dark QR code on white background always works best. Avoid light colors on light backgrounds — the scanner may not read it properly.
