Who Uses QR Codes and Why

QR codes have become an essential tool for businesses, freelancers, and individuals worldwide. Here are the most common use cases:

Restaurant & Café Menus

Many restaurants now use QR codes instead of printed menus. Create a URL QR code linking to your digital menu on Google Docs, Canva, or your website. Place it on tables and let customers scan to view the full menu on their phones.

Business Cards & Networking

Add a QR code to your business card that links to your LinkedIn profile, portfolio website, or contact page. When someone scans it, they immediately land on your professional profile — far more effective than typing a long URL.

WiFi Access in Hotels & Offices

Print a WiFi QR code at your reception or on room walls. Guests scan once and connect automatically no password typing required. Works with all Android and iOS devices.

UPI Payment Collection

Freelancers and small business owners in Pakistan and India use UPI QR codes to accept instant payments via Paytm, PhonePe, and Google Pay. Generate your QR code and display it at your counter or send it digitally.

Marketing Materials & Events

Add QR codes to flyers, posters, banners, and presentations. Link to your registration form, product page, YouTube video, or Instagram profile. Track engagement by using a URL shortener with analytics before generating the QR code.
