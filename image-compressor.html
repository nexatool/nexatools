Tips for Getting the Best Results

Each format has strengths here's how to choose the right one for your image:

1. WebP for the Web

WebP typically produces smaller files than JPG or PNG at similar visual quality, and supports transparency making it a great default for websites.

2. JPG for Wide Compatibility

If you need a format every app, printer, and platform accepts without question, JPG remains the safest choice for photographs.

3. PNG for Sharp Edges and Transparency

Logos, screenshots, and graphics with flat colors or transparency look best as PNG, though the files are typically larger than WebP.

4. Watch Transparency When Converting to JPG

Since JPG can't store transparency, any transparent pixels in your PNG or WebP source become solid white after conversion double-check the preview before downloading.
